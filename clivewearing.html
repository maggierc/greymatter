Clive Wearing

Clive is the most severe case of amnesia recorded in neuroscience. Throughout his life, doctors have scanned his brain and tested him to gain any information possible about his condition.

Background

Clive was born in the United Kingdom in 1938. He was known as a friendly, incredibly outgoing person.

As an adult, he worked as a conductor, teacher, and musicologist for the BBC.

He lived with his wife Deborah, up until 1985.

What Happened

In 1985, the brain infection began.

It first looked like a bad flu--he had a splitting headache, high fever, and couldn't sleep.

After a month, he was in a coma.

A rare infection had destroyed huge parts of his brain, especially the area for memory.

Symptoms

Clive lost his memory. Entirely.

He crossed out his handwriting because he didn't recognize it as his own.

His short-term memory could be erased with a blink of the eye.

He was otherwise fine, and sometimes became aware that he was living without any memories. It terrifies him when he realizes it.

Clive is the most severe case of amnesia recorded in neuroscience. Throughout his life, doctors have scanned his brain and tested him to gain any information possible about his condition.

The studies surrounding him vastly improved our knowledge of how memory operates in the brain. Documentaries about his life have shown light onto the tragedies that these conditions can cause.
