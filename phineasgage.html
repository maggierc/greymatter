Phineas Gage

Phineas is possibly the most famous case study in neuroscience. After surviving a massive injury to his brain, Phineas suffered a drastic change in personality.

Background

Phineas: born in 1823, living in Vermont.

Background

Phineas: born in 1823, living in Vermont.

What Happened

He was working on a railroad in Cavendish, Vermont.

During construction, an explosion caused a 3-foot iron bar to shoot straight through Phineas' head.

Workers rushed him to the hospital, and he miraculously survived. Even more incredibly, he could still walk, talk, and function in a normal way.

However, people soon saw that Phineas was a changed man.

What Happened

He was working on a railroad in Cavendish, Vermont.

During construction, an explosion caused a 3-foot iron bar to shoot straight through Phineas' head.

Workers rushed him to the hospital, and he miraculously survived. Even more incredibly, he could still walk, talk, and function in a normal way.

However, people soon saw that Phineas was a changed man.

Symptoms

The damage to Phineas' brain changed only one thing: his personality.

He went from "well-mannered" and "reliable" to coming off as a “foul-mouthed, ill-tempered, and irresponsible.”

Besides his behavior, he was perfectly functioning, and physically fine. The injury had changed him from a capable worker,

to an angry drunkard.

In the 1800s, scientists had no idea that our body's functions are located in specific parts of the brain. When Phineas suffered his injury, it completely changed our understanding of how the brain worked. Because of his case, scientists could determine exactly where personality is created in our minds.

The case of Phineas Gage is cited in hundreds of articles and modern textbooks, and continues to live on as a legend in psychology.
